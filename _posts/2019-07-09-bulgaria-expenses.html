Expenses breakdown for 18 days

Hannah and I spent 18 days in Bulgaria. Here is a general breakdown by category for two people.

  • Airbnb: £354
  • Co-working: £137 (10 day pass)
  • Eating out: £273
  • Groceries: £85
  • Drinks: £23
  • Coffee: £13
  • Gym: £11
  • Transport: £8

Total: £903

Average per day: £50

Total per person: £452

Average per person per day: £25

Our Airbnb

Our Airbnb was situated in the center of the town center. We couldn't have gotten a better location.

It was 5 minutes away from the town center and NDK and 20 minutes away from our co-working space.

There were also plenty of restaurants, bars and breakfast choices, so I never felt that I'm missing something

Here is a listing of the apartment on Airbnb - highly recommend it if you are staying in the city.

Co-working and cafés

Our co-working space was called Soho - a big 3 story artsy house situated in the town center. It included both offices and hot-desking.

It was disappointing to see that the space focused more on offices than hot-desking/permanent desks. There was only one area of the house (plus the garden) which was designated for co-working, which probably has a capacity of about 20 people.

The space didn't have a lot of free spaces, and the space was often noisy and not well air-conditioned.

Next time when I'm back in Sofia, I will explore different co-working spaces such as Betahouse.

I also found several coffee shops where I worked often.

There are plenty of laptop friendly coffee shops. They weren't too busy as well!

What did we do?

Living in Sofia: we didn't do much touristy stuff in Sofia, as I have been here many times, so did Hannah one time about 2 years ago.

I also started my full-time job whenever right when I came to Bulgaria, so I needed some time to adjust to this change.

On the first day we came, we stumbled on a concert in the town center.

We also did some walking around the town center

During the weekend, I had a chance to catch up with my brother and sister

Visiting Shumen: over the weekend, we visited my hometown Shumen, which was 7 hours on the train from Sofia.
